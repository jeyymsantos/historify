SALAMANDER by Atoy Llave

The Salamander, which is the creation of Atoy Llave of A-Toy Body Kits, a Filipino custom vehicle 'Sifu' or master, came to life after he shared his ideas with well-known boat builder Dominic Chung. Following the exchange of ideas, Llave and Chung founded a collaboration called H2O Technologies.

SALT LIGHT by Aisa and Raphael Mijeno

Aisa and Raphael Mijeno of the Philippines designed the SALt light, which stands for "Sustainable Alternative Lighting." A galvanic interaction between an anode and saline waterpower the lamp. SALt is based on metal-air technology where it consumes ambient air as cathode, metal anode and saline solution to create a chemical reaction. This generates enough power to light up LEDs and charge low-power mobile devices like cell phones.

OL MOSQUITO TRAP by DOST

The female mosquito lays its eggs after her blood meal is fulfilled or completed. This is when the OL mosquito trap stops mosquitoes from reproducing. Female mosquitoes are attracted to the OL mosquito trap device and lay their eggs on it. The female mosquitoes are attracted to the black paint or container. In addition, the strip of moist "lawanit" board inside the trap creates an ideal environment for female mosquitoes, increasing their attraction.

SARAO MOTORS by Leonardo Salvador Sarao Sr.

Sarao Motors, a firm noted for creating, manufacturing, and marketing the jeepney, the most common method of transportation in the Philippines, was founded and owned by Leonardo Salvador Sarao Sr. He made E-Jeepney. He isn't the only person to tinker with excess Jeeps left behind from the US military. He regarded the army as an economic opportunity for mass transportation after WWII.

BANANA KETSUP by Maria Ylagan Orosa

Maria Ylagan Orosa, a food scientist, is credited with developing banana ketchup. During the 1930s, Orosa worked to strengthen the Philippines by developing meals that might substitute popular imported cuisine. Her commitment to using locally sourced products led her to reimagine ketchup, which formerly relied on scarce tomatoes, and replace them with bountiful bananas. The Universal Food Corporation (UFC), currently known as the Universal Food Corporation, created and sold the first commercially mass-produced banana ketchup in 1942.

MEDICAL INCUBATOR by Dr. Fe Del Mundo

Dr. Fe del Mundo created a bamboo version of a modern medical incubator that is less expensive and easy to assemble for rural populations. Premature babies delivered before the mother's 37th week of pregnancy are given it. Dr. del Mundo was the first female pediatrician to be accepted into Harvard Medical School in 1936. In 1980, she became the first woman to be named a National Scientist in the Philippines. In addition, she founded the Children's Medical Center. She died in 2011, just a few days before her 100th birthday.
